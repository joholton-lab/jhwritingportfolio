Using loyalty programs to boost customer retention and lifetime value

A full end-to-end tutorial showing merchants how to configure milestone rewards and punch card flows in Recharge — combining strategic context, step-by-step instructions, and decision-making guidance.

Use Recharge Rewards to reward customers at key order milestones and to show progress, boosting customer retention and lifetime value.

This tutorial shows you how to set up milestone rewards and a punch card flow that work together to support long-term subscription retention.

Introduction

Milestone rewards and punch cards help you encourage repeat subscription purchases by rewarding customers at specific order milestones and showing them their progress along the way.

Using Rewards flows, you can trigger rewards — such as free gifts, credits, or discounts — when a customer reaches a set subscription order number (for example, order 3, 5, or 10). A punch card flow works alongside these rewards by making progress visible in the customer portal, so customers know how close they are to earning their next reward.

This approach matters because subscription churn often happens at predictable points. By rewarding customers at key moments and clearly communicating what's coming next, you give them a concrete reason to stay subscribed. Merchants commonly use milestone rewards and punch cards to increase repeat purchases, improve retention at early drop-off points, and grow customer lifetime value by turning loyalty into a clear, goal-driven experience.

Prerequisites

You must have access to Rewards to follow this tutorial. Access to Rewards depends on your plan. Check the Loyalty tab in your merchant portal to confirm whether this feature is available in your store.

Things to consider

Choose the right incentive type for your business. When selecting an incentive, consider your margins, fulfillment complexity, and customer behavior. Free gifts, credits, and percentage-based discounts can all be effective milestone rewards, but each comes with trade-offs:

  • Free gifts often feel high-value to customers while typically costing less than percentage-based discounts. However, they can complicate fulfillment and may be sent even if a customer doesn't want the item.
  • Credits require customers to actively apply the reward, helping ensure you're not providing value to customers who don't engage. Credits often surface when customers consider canceling, making them a strong retention option with no additional fulfillment overhead.
  • Percentage-based discounts apply automatically and are easy to understand, but they directly impact margins and may be redeemed even when they're not needed to influence retention.

Monitor your Cohort performance dashboard. Use this dashboard to identify where customers tend to drop off, and evaluate whether your milestone rewards and punch card program are improving retention at those points.

Start simple and test before adding complexity. Launch with a single milestone reward and use A/B testing or retention metrics to measure impact before layering in additional milestones or more complex flows.

Setup instructions

Step 1 — Review your Cohort Performance dashboard

The Cohort performance dashboard is your go-to resource for understanding how customers engage with their subscriptions and where they're most likely to cancel. Before building any flows, review this dashboard to identify subscription order numbers with the highest drop-off rates.

  1. In the Recharge merchant portal, select Analytics and then click Dashboards.
  2. From the Dashboard menu, select Customers and then choose the Cohort performance dashboard.
  3. Select Subscriber retention by order number from the drop-down menu.
  4. Review the heatmap to identify subscription order drop-off points.
  5. Optional — Select Add product to filter the dashboard by a specific product.
Tip Recharge data shows that customers often drop off after order 3, making it a strong candidate for milestone rewards.

Step 2 — Build your Milestone subscription purchase flows

After reviewing the Cohort performance dashboard and identifying which subscription orders to reward, you're ready to build your Rewards flows. To offer rewards at different order milestones, you'll create multiple Milestone subscription purchase Rewards flows — one for each order point you want to incentivize.

Step 2.1 — Access the flow builder

Click Loyalty in the Recharge merchant portal and select Rewards. Click Create from template and select Milestone subscription purchase.

Step 2.2 — Review the flow trigger

The flow trigger determines when the flow starts. By default, the trigger uses the streak count for subscription orders condition. Change the operator to Equals and enter the streak count for your milestone (for example, enter 3 to reward after three consecutive orders).

Step 2.3 — Set the incentive

Choose the reward customers receive. Recharge recommends free gifts or credits as milestone incentives. Configure the incentive node, then save and test the flow before activating.

Warning Do not activate your milestone flows until you've created all flows, including the punch card flow outlined in Step 3.

Step 3 — Build your punch card flow

After building your milestone flows, use the Recharge Punch Card flow to build excitement in the customer portal by showing customers how many orders remain before they reach each milestone.

  1. Click Loyalty and select Rewards.
  2. Click Create from template and select Punch card banner.
  3. Build conditional branches to tailor messaging to each customer's streak count.
  4. Update announcement nodes for each branch to reflect the customer's milestone progress.
  5. Save the flow. Activate only after all milestone flows are ready.

Step 4 — Activate and announce

When ready, activate both the milestone rewards flows and the punch card banner flow. Then promote your program through email, SMS, and on-site banners so customers understand how to earn rewards.

What's next

  • Monitor your Cohort performance dashboard to compare retention before and after launch.
  • Identify drop-off points where customers may still disengage.
  • Adjust milestone incentives or punch card rewards to improve retention at key moments.
  • Continue refining your program based on cohort trends and customer behavior.
