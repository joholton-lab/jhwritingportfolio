Using loyalty programs to boost customer retention and lifetime value

A full end-to-end tutorial showing merchants how to configure milestone rewards and punch card flows in Recharge — combining strategic context, step-by-step instructions, and decision-making guidance.

Use Recharge Rewards to reward customers at key order milestones and to show progress, boosting customer retention and lifetime value.

This tutorial shows you how to set up milestone rewards and a punch card flow that work together to support long-term subscription retention.

Introduction

Milestone rewards and punch cards help you encourage repeat subscription purchases by rewarding customers at specific order milestones and showing them their progress along the way.

Using Rewards flows, you can trigger rewards — such as free gifts, credits, or discounts — when a customer reaches a set subscription order number (for example, order 3, 5, or 10). A punch card flow works alongside these rewards by making progress visible in the customer portal, so customers know how close they are to earning their next reward.

This approach matters because subscription churn often happens at predictable points. By rewarding customers at key moments and clearly communicating what's coming next, you give them a concrete reason to stay subscribed. Merchants commonly use milestone rewards and punch cards to increase repeat purchases, improve retention at early drop-off points, and grow customer lifetime value by turning loyalty into a clear, goal-driven experience.

Prerequisites

You must have access to Rewards to follow this tutorial. Access to Rewards depends on your plan. Check the Loyalty tab in your merchant portal to confirm whether this feature is available in your store.

Things to consider

Choose the right incentive type for your business. When selecting an incentive, consider your margins, fulfillment complexity, and customer behavior. Free gifts, credits, and percentage-based discounts can all be effective milestone rewards, but each comes with trade-offs:

  • Free gifts often feel high-value to customers while typically costing less than percentage-based discounts. However, they can complicate fulfillment and may be sent even if a customer doesn't want the item.
  • Credits require customers to actively apply the reward, helping ensure you're not providing value to customers who don't engage. Credits often surface when customers consider canceling, making them a strong retention option with no additional fulfillment overhead.
  • Percentage-based discounts apply automatically and are easy to understand, but they directly impact margins and may be redeemed even when they're not needed to influence retention.

Monitor your Cohort performance dashboard. Use this dashboard to identify where customers tend to drop off, and evaluate whether your milestone rewards and punch card program are improving retention at those points.

Start simple and test before adding complexity. Launch with a single milestone reward and use A/B testing or retention metrics to measure impact before layering in additional milestones or more complex flows.

Setup instructions

Step 1 — Review your Cohort Performance dashboard

The Cohort performance dashboard is your go-to resource for understanding how customers engage with their subscriptions and where they're most likely to cancel. Before building any flows, review this dashboard to identify subscription order numbers with the highest drop-off rates. You can then use these insights to create milestone reward flows that incentivize customers at exactly the right time.

  1. In the Recharge merchant portal, select Analytics and then click Dashboards.
  2. From the Dashboard menu, select Customers and then choose the Cohort performance dashboard.
  3. Select Subscriber retention by order number from the drop-down menu.
  4. Review the heatmap to identify subscription order drop-off points.
  5. Optional — Select Add product to filter the dashboard by a specific product.

Each column in the heatmap corresponds to a recurring order number, and darker shading indicates stronger retention. Look for order numbers where retention consistently drops across cohorts. These points often signal when customers are most likely to cancel and where milestone rewards can have the greatest impact. Use this data to determine when to offer incentives to customers to prevent churn.

Tip Recharge data shows that customers often drop off after order 3, making it a strong candidate for milestone rewards.

Step 2 — Build your Milestone subscription purchase flows

After reviewing the Cohort performance dashboard and identifying which subscription orders to reward, you're ready to build your Rewards flows. To offer rewards at different order milestones, you'll create multiple Milestone subscription purchase Rewards flows — one for each order point you want to incentivize.

Step 2.1 — Access the flow builder

  1. Click Loyalty in the Recharge merchant portal and select Rewards.
  2. Click Create from template and select Milestone subscription purchase.
  3. Click Manage settings to name and describe your flow, set run limits, and choose the recently processed order option so rewards are only granted for completed purchases.
  4. Click Apply to save your settings.

Step 2.2 — Review the flow trigger

The flow trigger determines when the flow starts and ensures it applies only to customers who meet your specific conditions.

  1. Click on the Trigger node in the flow canvas.
  2. By default, the trigger uses the streak count for subscription orders condition — targeting customers based on consecutive orders without cancellations.
  3. Change the operator from is greater than or equal to to Equals.
  4. Enter the streak count for your milestone. For example, enter 3 to reward customers after three consecutive orders.
  5. Click Apply to save your changes.
Tip Recharge recommends starting with a simple trigger before adding additional conditions. After gathering data, you can refine targeting for specific customer segments.

Step 2.3 — Set the incentive

Choose the reward customers receive when they hit a milestone. Recharge recommends free gifts or credits.

Free gifts

  1. Click on the Incentive node in the flow canvas.
  2. Set the Incentive type to One-time product.
  3. Click Select product and choose the free product customers receive.
  4. Set the order skip behavior to Keep incentive on order skip so customers still receive their reward after skipping.
  5. Click Apply to save your changes.

Credits

  1. On the Incentive node, click the three vertical dot menu and select Delete.
  2. Drag the Issue credit node from the Add a node menu to replace it — place it between the Trigger and Segment nodes.
  3. Choose a credit type: Fixed amount or Percentage.
  4. Enter the credit amount and set an expiration option: use store settings, a custom timeframe, or never expire.
  5. Click Apply to save your changes.

Step 2.4 — Configure a segment

  1. Click on the Segment node in the flow canvas.
  2. Choose Add to segment and select or create a segment for customers who enter the flow.
  3. Click Apply to save your changes.

Step 2.5 — Create the notification

Notifying customers about their incentive helps ensure the reward feels valuable. When customers know they've earned something, they're more likely to notice it, understand why they received it, and feel recognized for their loyalty.

  1. Click on the Notification node in the flow canvas.
  2. Set the language, sender name, email, and subject line.
  3. Click Edit email body to customize the notification — use variables to display dynamic content.
  4. Click Preview to confirm it appears as intended, then select Apply.
Tip No HTML experience? Use tools like W3's HTML Online Editor or AI tools like ChatGPT to create polished notifications without coding.

Step 2.6 — Save, test, and repeat

  1. Click Save on the flow canvas.
  2. Click the three vertical dots menu and select Test Flow.
  3. Enter a test customer's email or ID, then click Run flow.
  4. Click View results to confirm the incentive was added to the customer's next upcoming order.
  5. Repeat Steps 2.1–2.6 for each additional milestone you want to reward.
Warning Do not activate your milestone flows until you've created all flows, including the punch card flow outlined in Step 3. Milestone rewards and punch cards work best together — milestone flows provide incentives, while the punch card flow keeps customers motivated by showing progress.

Step 3 — Build your punch card flow

After building your milestone flows, use the Recharge Punch Card flow to build excitement in the customer portal by showing customers how many orders remain before they reach each milestone.

Step 3.1 — Access the flow builder

  1. Click Loyalty and select Rewards.
  2. Click Create from template and select Punch card banner.
  3. Click Manage settings to name and describe your flow.

Step 3.2 — Build your conditional branches

The Punch card banner flow uses conditional branches to tailor messaging to each customer's streak count. For example, if customers earn a free gift after order 3:

  • Streak 2: Tell customers they are one order away from a free gift.
  • Streak 4: Tell customers a free gift will be added to their next order.
  1. Click on the Conditional branch node.
  2. Edit or add branches using the subscription orders equals condition, setting the streak count to match each milestone.
  3. Click Apply to save your changes.
Tip Consider creating a branch for each streak number to track progress at every order and tailor messaging to the customer's journey.

Step 3.3 — Update announcements and save

  1. Click the Announcement node for each conditional branch.
  2. Choose Text and image or Custom HTML and CSS to customize the banner.
  3. Add a heading and body text that reflects the customer's current milestone progress.
  4. Click Done, then Apply to save.
  5. Repeat for each branch, then click Save on the flow canvas.

Step 4 — Optional: Review Shopify shipping settings

If you're offering free gifts, add the free gift product to the correct shipping profile in Shopify so customers are not charged shipping fees when they receive their reward.

Step 5 — Activate and announce

When ready, activate both your milestone rewards flows and the punch card banner flow. Then promote your program through email, SMS, and on-site banners so customers understand how to earn rewards and which incentives are available. Clear communication helps drive engagement and encourages customers to participate early.

What's next

  • Monitor your Cohort performance dashboard to compare retention before and after launch.
  • Identify drop-off points where customers may still disengage.
  • Adjust milestone incentives or punch card rewards to improve retention at key moments.
  • Continue refining your program based on cohort trends and customer behavior.

Affinity utilizes events to broadcast crucial information, offering insights into user behavior and enabling the creation of synchronized custom content extensions.

Below is an overview of the various event types supported by Affinity and how they can be leveraged.

Before you start

Note This article only applies if you use the Affinity Home Page Builder to customize the Affinity customer portal. See Leveraging events in Affinity if you are using the previous version of Affinity.

Available events

Event type Purpose
Click events Initiated by user actions, primarily indicating intent. The click event is triggered at the initial step — for example, when a user clicks Skip to begin skipping an order.
Action events Signify the finalization of an action. In the order skip example, the action event fires when Recharge actually skips the order — the last step in the flow.
Navigation events Emitted every time a user navigates to a new page in Affinity. Used to understand user engagement and navigation patterns in the portal.
Directives Listened to, rather than emitted by Affinity. These allow custom extensions to trigger specific behaviors within Affinity.

Example — skipping an order fires three sequential events:

  1. The user clicks Skip. → Click event
  2. The user confirms by clicking Yes, skip this order. → Intermediate event
  3. Recharge skips the order. → Action event

Practical application

Create custom flows

Use Affinity to craft user experiences that meet your unique business needs. You can intercept the default behavior when a customer initiates an action — such as skipping, rescheduling, or cancelling — and instead initiate a custom flow.

The following example shows how to add a custom flow when a customer initiates the reschedule action:

<script>
document.addEventListener("Recharge::click::reschedule", (event) => {
  // Display custom reschedule UI
});
</script>

Analytics

Use events to transmit data to your preferred analytics tools and gain insight into user behavior:

  • Use Click and Action events to observe completion rates for actions like skipping, rescheduling, or ordering immediately — by comparing the initial click (intent) against the completion of the action.
  • Use Navigation events to analyze user engagement and map out customer journeys within the portal, identifying popular paths and potential areas for improvement.

Customer dashboards

A structured reference guide covering four interconnected analytics dashboards — explaining what each dashboard does, how to interpret it, and which metrics to prioritize. Combines conceptual framing with practical "where to start" guidance for merchant audiences.

Overview Customer Overview Churn Actions Cohort Performance

The Customer dashboards help you understand whether your subscriber base is growing, how churn is impacting revenue, and which customer behaviors influence long-term retention. Together, these dashboards tell the story of your subscriber lifecycle — from acquisition to churn — so you can identify growth opportunities and retention risks early.

Before you start Customer dashboards are refreshed daily by 8 AM ET. To access Analytics dashboards, make sure your account has both the Analytics and Home permissions enabled.

Overview

Use the Customer dashboards to understand:

  • Whether your subscriber base is growing or declining
  • What type of churn is occurring
  • How customers manage their subscriptions
  • How subscriber behavior impacts long-term revenue

Together, these dashboards help you track the full subscriber lifecycle: Acquisition (new subscribers) → Engagement (actions and behavior) → Retention (churn trends) → Long-term value (cohort performance).

Where to start

  1. Open the Customer Overview dashboard to confirm whether subscriber growth is positive.
  2. Check the Customer churn dashboard to understand what is driving losses.
  3. Use the Actions dashboard to identify behavior trends that may signal future churn.
  4. Review the Cohort Performance dashboard to evaluate long-term retention and revenue quality.

Access the Customer dashboards

  1. Click Analytics in your merchant portal.
  2. Select Dashboards.
  3. Choose a report from the Customers drop-down menu.

Customer Overview dashboard

The Customer Overview dashboard shows the size and trend of your active and churned subscriber base over time. Use it to evaluate subscriber growth and overall retention performance within a selected time period.

Active subscribers are customers with at least one active subscription.

Use this dashboard to Track subscriber growth or decline · Monitor churn alongside acquisition · Compare new vs reactivated growth · Evaluate marketing campaign impact · Understand net subscriber movement

How to review trends

  1. Start with Total active subscribers to identify overall growth direction.
  2. Review Net gained/lost to determine whether acquisition is outpacing churn.
  3. Compare New vs Reactivated subscribers to understand growth sources.
  4. Review Churned subscribers to identify whether losses are offsetting gains.

Filters

  • Date Range — Sets the time frame for your data.
  • Date Granularity — Groups data by day, week, month, or quarter.
  • Product filter — Filters metrics to specific products or variants.
  • Segment filter — Filters metrics to a selected customer segment.

Customer churn dashboard

The Customer churn dashboard shows churn frequency and churn type. Use it to monitor retention performance and evaluate revenue impact.

Use this dashboard to Measure overall churn rate · Analyze churn by type (active, passive, expired) · Compare churn across time periods · Evaluate revenue lost to churn (Churned MRR) · Assess the impact of pricing or retention changes

How to review churn trends

  1. Start with the overall Churn rate trend to identify spikes or sustained increases.
  2. Use Compare date range to evaluate acceleration or improvement.
  3. Review churn by type to determine whether losses are driven by cancellations, payment failures, or expirations.
  4. Check Churned MRR to measure revenue impact, not just subscriber count.

Filters

  • Date Range — Sets the time frame for your data.
  • Compare date range — Sets a past time frame to compare against your selected range.
  • Display — Groups data by day, week, month, or quarter.
  • Product filter — Views subscription churn per product.
  • Segment filter — Filters metrics to a selected customer segment.

Actions dashboard

The Actions dashboard shows how customers manage their subscriptions and where those actions occur — for example, in the customer portal or the merchant portal. Use it to identify behavior patterns and potential churn signals.

Use this dashboard to Monitor skips, swaps, pauses, and reactivations · Identify behaviors that may signal future churn · Evaluate engagement with discounts and add-ons · Compare action volume across sources · Measure the impact of retention initiatives

How to review subscriber behavior

  1. Review overall action volume trends to identify behavioral shifts.
  2. Monitor Pauses and frequent Skips as potential churn indicators.
  3. Evaluate Reactivations and discount usage to assess retention effectiveness.
  4. Compare action sources to understand where customers manage subscriptions.

Filters

  • Date Range — Sets the time frame for your data.
  • Compare date range — Sets a past time frame to compare against your selected range.
  • Display — Groups data by day, week, month, or quarter.
  • Action source — Displays actions by source: API, Customer portal, Merchant portal, Recharge admin, or Other.

Cohort Performance dashboard

The Cohort Performance dashboard evaluates long-term subscriber quality by tracking retention, revenue, and engagement over time. Use it to measure lifetime value and compare acquisition performance across periods or products.

This dashboard also includes a Compare cohorts view, which lets you compare the weighted average of any metric across a specified range for different products or segments — up to 10 products across any cohort KPI.

Use this dashboard to Measure early- and long-term retention by cohort · Evaluate lifetime subscription revenue trends · Compare newer cohorts to historical performance · Identify high-retention products or segments · Assess acquisition quality and long-term ROI

How to review cohort performance

  1. Start with Subscriber retention by month, focusing on Months 1–3.
  2. Review cumulative revenue metrics to evaluate lifetime value.
  3. Compare cohorts across time periods to identify performance shifts.
  4. Apply product or segment filters to isolate strong or weak performers.

Compare cohorts

  1. Click Analytics and select Customers in your Recharge merchant portal.
  2. Select the Cohort Performance tab.
  3. Click the Compare cohorts dropdown.
  4. Select the products you want to compare and click Apply to confirm.

Filters

  • Metric filter — Selects the cohort-based metric to analyze, including retention, revenue, subscription orders, and lifetime value metrics.
  • Cohort date range — Sets the time frame for your data.
  • Product filter — Views subscription churn per product.
  • Segment filter — Filters metrics to a selected customer segment.

Understanding the Trigger node for Automate flows

A conceptual guide explaining how a complex feature works, why it matters, and how to use it effectively — combining feature explanation, AND/OR logic guidance, a use case table, and edge case considerations.

+
Written for Recharge Payments — names and proprietary details used with context
Before you start How it works Configure Use cases Considerations

The Trigger node ensures that your flows apply only to specific customers or orders, enabling you to provide a personalized experience.

This guide explains how to leverage conditions in the Trigger node for Automate flows, along with use cases and considerations.

Platform Shopify Checkout Integration · Migrated Shopify Checkout Integration

Before you start

Note This article only applies to Automate flows. The Trigger node for other flows offers a different set of conditions.

Trigger conditions control which orders can enter an Automate flow. To avoid unintentionally running a flow on every processed order, decide what you want to target before building your flow.

  • Which product(s) the customer must purchase (for example, a starter kit SKU).
  • When the flow should apply (for example, only on a customer's first recurring order).
  • Who should qualify (for example, customers with specific tags or in a specific segment).
Tip If your flow is intended to run for a broad audience, use caution. Overly broad trigger conditions can cause your flow to apply to more orders than expected.

How it works

Trigger conditions are entry rules for Automate flows. They determine which orders are allowed to enter a flow and begin running through the canvas.

Trigger conditions help you:

  • Run Automate only on specific orders instead of every processed order.
  • Reduce complex branching used only for filtering.
  • Build smaller, more intentional automations with clearer targeting.

Trigger conditions also help you avoid overly complex flows, excessive branching, and workarounds that are hard to debug and maintain.

Note Existing flows are not affected. Trigger conditions do not change how currently active flows execute unless you edit and save updated conditions.

Configure trigger conditions

  1. In your Recharge merchant portal, click Products and select Automate.
  2. Create a new flow from a template, or open an existing flow.
  3. Click the Trigger node.
  4. Click Add to add trigger conditions.
  5. Save your changes.

Use AND/OR logic to fine-tune targeting

  • Use AND when all conditions must be met.
  • Use OR when any of the condition groups can be met.

Example

(Line item contains Starter Kit) AND (Order number equals 3)

OR (Customer tag equals VIP)

Available trigger conditions

  • Subscription conditions — Target specific subscription details such as product, interval, or order milestones.
  • Customer conditions — Target customers based on attributes such as tags, segments, or subscription activity.
  • Processed charge conditions — Target order-level details like charge date, total price, or discount code.
  • Processed charge line item conditions — Target specific products or variants within a processed charge.
  • Shipping address conditions — Target orders based on the delivery location.

Use cases

Use case Trigger conditions and canvas actions
Update subscription frequency after a milestone Trigger condition: Customer condition — Number of subscription orders processed
Canvas action: Subscription update node to modify frequency.
Swap a starter kit to refills on Order 3 Trigger conditions: Line item — Processed charge line item (target kit product) + Customer — Streak count for subscription orders (target order milestone)
Canvas action: Subscription node with the Swap action.
Add a delay before taking action Trigger condition: Line item — Processed charge line item (target qualifying product)
Canvas actions: Delay node, followed by a Subscription update node.

Considerations

Consideration Details
Existing flows aren't impacted automatically There are no changes to how currently active flows execute. Trigger conditions can now be viewed and edited on existing flows.
Warning modal for overly broad conditions If your trigger conditions appear overly broad, Recharge displays a warning modal when you save. The warning does not block activation — it's designed to ensure you understand the potential impact before saving.
